Thank you for looking me up! I have a wide array of passions and interests, and I hope this portfolio will help you learn more about me as an employee and collaborator on projects.I have a background in legal research and enjoy projects that unite my passions for history and law. I have worked on a number of exhibits using archival and historical collections including an online archive of material from Michigan suffragette Lucia Grimes and continuity stills from Orson Welles Citizen Kane. I would love to contribute to other digitization and archival projects in the future. I have worked in a number of offices and venues where I have built skills in leadership, project development, managment, presentation, legal work and much more. In the future I hope to combine my love of law and history pursing a crareer as a general counsel memeber in a museum.

Emily Finch is a candidate for her Masters in Information at the University of Michigan School of Information. In addition she is pursuing a Graduate Certificate in Museum Studies at the University of Michigan Rackham Graduate School. Upon completion of these degrees in 2019 she plans on attending law school and beginning a career in cultural heritage law. Prior to attending the University of Michigan Emily Finch completed her Bachelors of Arts at Kalamazoo College where she majored in English and History, minored in Political Science and concentrated in American Studies.For a period of her undergraduate study Emily Finch completed course work at the University of Aberdeen in Aberdeen, Scotland taking courses in contemporary British literature, Spanish politics, and Russian History.In her free time, Emily enjoys traveling the globe, reading classical literature, baking, visiting museums, and volunteering.
